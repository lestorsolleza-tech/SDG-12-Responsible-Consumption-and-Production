Sustainable Consumption on Food, Energy, and Water

SDG 12 aims to ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns by 2030. This site focuses on three key targets: Food (reducing waste), Energy (efficient use), and Water (conservation). According to the UN SDG Report 2023, unsustainable consumption contributes to 45% of global greenhouse gas emissions.

Global Statistics on SDG 12 Targets (2024-2025 Data)

Food Waste

1.05 billion tonnes of food waste were generated globally in 2022 (≈132 kg per person), representing ~19% of food available to consumers. About 60% of this waste (631 million tonnes) occurred at the household level.

Source: UNEP Food Waste Index Report 2024, published March 27, 2024

Energy & Emissions

Global energy-related CO₂ emissions reached 37.8 Gt in 2024 (up 0.8% from 2023). Renewables and nuclear provided 40% of global electricity for the first time, but fossil fuels still dominate energy mix.

Source: IEA Global Energy Review 2025, published March 2025
View Report →

Water Access

2.1 billion people (26% of global population) still lacked safely managed drinking water services as of 2024, including 106 million who drink directly from surface water sources.

Source: WHO/UNICEF JMP Report 2025, published August 26, 2025
