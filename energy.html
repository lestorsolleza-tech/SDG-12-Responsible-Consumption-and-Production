Energy Efficiency & Conservation

Efficient energy use is essential for reducing carbon emissions, saving natural resources, and supporting global sustainability goals under SDG 12.

2024 Global Update: Energy-related CO₂ emissions reached 37.8 Gt in 2024 (up 0.8% from 2023). For the first time, renewables and nuclear provided 40% of global electricity. Advanced economies saw emissions decline to 50-year lows.
Source: IEA Global Energy Review 2025, published March 2025 | View Report →

Why Energy Conservatoin Matters?

Preserving energy doesn't just reduce electricity bills-it helps combat climate change, improves air quality, and decrease reliance on fussil fuels. every individual and business place a role in reducing energy demand by adopting smarter and more efficient practices such us using renewable energy, reducing consumption, and optimizing daily energy usage.

Local Research Finding:Studies near WVSU Janiuay Campus show that 68% of households practice baisc energy-saving habits like turning off lights and unplugging unused appliances. However, only 34% use energy-effiecient devices such us LED bulbs or inverter appliances, with cost cited as the main barrier

Actions to Conserve and Use Energy Efficiently

  • Switch to LED bulbs and energy-efficient appliances to reduce household energy use by 20–30%.
  • Install solar panels or participate in community renewable energy programs.
  • Use energy-saving settings on computers, AC units, and refrigerators.
  • Opt for public transportation, cycling, or carpooling to reduce fuel consumption.
  • Conduct energy audits in homes and industries to identify wasteful areas.
  • Use smart meters and smart home systems to monitor and control energy usage.
  • Improve insulation and seal air leaks to reduce heating and cooling losses.
  • Adopt eco-friendly building design such as passive cooling and natural lighting.

Key Findings from 2024

Emissions Growth Slowing

Global energy CO₂ emissions grew only 0.8% in 2024—significantly slower than the 1.1% average over the past decade, thanks to clean energy deployment.

Clean Energy Impact

Without clean technologies, emissions would have been 3x higher. Solar PV and wind are now avoiding ~2.3 Gt CO₂ annually.

Regional Differences

Advanced economies hit 50-year low emissions (-1.1%), while emerging markets in Asia saw increases due to economic growth and coal reliance.

Practical Energy-Saving Tips

  • Turn off lights when leaving a room.
  • Unplug chargers and devices not in use.
  • Wash clothes with cold water to reduce heating energy.
  • Use natural daylight whenever possible.
  • Set air conditioners to 24°C (75°F) for optimal efficiency.
  • Limit the use of high-energy devices during peak hours.
  • Replace old appliances with Energy Star certified models.
  • Use power strips to easily cut power to multiple devices.

Understanding Energy Efficiency

Visual guides to help you make informed energy choices:

Energy Efficiency Labels & Ratings

Labels help you compare appliances and buildings. Look for high ratings (A+++ in EU, Energy Star in US) to choose appliances that save energy and money.

Energy Audits

An energy audit examines how energy is used and where it is wasted. It pinpoints inefficiencies and suggests improvements like better insulation or efficient appliances.

Smart Energy Monitoring Tools

Digital tools that track and visualize energy use. They help identify high-usage times, wasteful habits, and opportunities for savings.

Why Energy Efficiency Matters for SDG 12

Energy efficiency is central to responsible consumption and production. It reduces global warming, lowers environmental pollution, and saves billions of dollars in energy costs.

  • Environmental Impact: Reduces greenhouse gas emissions and air pollution.
  • Economic Benefits: Lowers utility bills for households and operational costs for businesses.
  • Energy Security: Reduces dependence on imported fossil fuels.
  • Social Impact: Creates healthier living conditions and green jobs.
  • Long-Term Sustainability: Preserves resources for future generations.

Eco-Friendly Habits & Lifestyle Adjustments

Small daily changes contribute to significant energy savings:

  • Adopt minimalist habits — consume only what you need
  • Buy energy-efficient products — look for certifications and ratings
  • Choose reusable items — reduce single-use products that require energy to produce
  • Support renewable energy — opt for green electricity plans where available
  • Educate others — share energy-saving tips with family and community
